The Broadback River

By motorboat we visited this mighty river that flows into James Bay. We saw ducks and a bald eagle. Cree seek to protect this river valley from loggers, and blocked a bridge across the Broadback.

Lake Quenonisca

On our way back, Cree hunters shot a 300-kilogram moose in the lake.

Waswanipi

This First Nations community's Cree name means light in the water. "In the olden days guys used to light a fire by the shore and the fish would come to the shallow part of the water," says Don Saganash, a Cree tallyman.

Roberval

This town is a hub known for its hospital in Quebec's thriving Lac Saint-Jean region, which is home to 300,000 people. We lunched with our guides from Resolute Forest Products at Le Coq Roti (The Roasted Rooster).

Logging operations in Mistasibi

About 100 kilometres north of the logging camp we visited a spot where the Fillion family operated two harvesters and a forwarder, big machines which cut the forest 24 hours a day.

Next ↓

Dolbeau-Mistassini

In this sawmill and paper mill town on Lac Saint-Jean we met Jean-Pierre Boivin, mayor of the Maria-Chapdelaine county. The size of Sweden, Maria-Chapelaine is home to 8,000 lakes and depends on logging for its livelyhood.

Mashteuiatsh

The Pekuakamiulnuatsh, an Innu tribe who were nomadic for many years, live in this community on the shore of Lac Saint-Jean, formerly known as Pointe-Bleue. They supply logs to Resolute's mills.

Montreal

In Montreal we visited the head office of Resolute Forest Products to interview Richard Garneau, the chief executive. Garneau, somewhat hopefully perhaps, has on his wall a peace pipe he bought in Mashteuiatsh.
